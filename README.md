# Formulate an LLC
You want to establish a "corporate veil" between your business and personal assets. Formulating an LLC puts a legal wall between you and your company. It also allows you to operate on a more professional level and treat the buiness like a business.
## Formation process
Should be a very simple excercise depending on the state. The state of Iowa is a $50 filing fee with an biennial report that must be filed for around $50.
* Use your own attorney
* Use an online company like [Legal Zoom](https://www.legalzoom.com/)
# Open a Business checking and savings account
You want to establish a clear separation of your business assets and your personal assets.
* You want to make sure that any personal assets are not at risk if someone comes after your business assets
* You want to make sure that any business assets are not at risk if someone comes after your personal assets
## Choosing a bank
* Consider a local bank. You may have future banking needs and it could be advantageous to build a more personal, face to face relationship. Of course, for the puporses of holding your money, an online bank works just fine.
* You may also want to consider opening with a company that is separate from your personal assets. You would not want to get into the situation where there debts in your personal life that the bank would access your business funds (which they can do if they are co-located) or vice versa.
